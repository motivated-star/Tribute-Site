A Tribute to Vikram Batra 1974-1999

"Our beloved India, pays a tribute to our Indian Army Officer, who was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra, The highest Indian military decoration, for his actions during the Kargil War in 1999."

Vikram Batra PVC (9 September 1974 â€“ 7 July 1999) was an Indian Army officer. He was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra, the highest Indian military decoration, for his actions during the Kargil War.

Vikram Batra was awarded the Param Vir Chakra, India's highest military honour on 15 August 1999, the 52nd anniversary of India's independence. His father G.L. Batra received the honour for his deceased son from the President of India, the late K. R. Narayanan.The Kargil war fought on the Himalayan peaks is well-known, and Captain Vikram Batra, who played a key part in its victory, likewise requires no introduction. When Pakistan unlawfully conquered much of the heights of Kargil in 1999, the Indian Army began Operation Vijay to reclaim the summits, with Captain Vikram Batra playing a key role. For his courage, Captain Vikram Batra was posthumously awarded India's highest and most prestigious medal, the Param Vir Chakra.
